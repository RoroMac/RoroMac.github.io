Data voids as coined by Golebiewski and Boyd (2019) are pockets on the internet where very little information available causes disproportionately high overrepresentation of relevant but unreliable sources. While data voids themselves are not inherently problematic, the lack of competing information magnifies any content that enters; opening potential for misinformation, conspiracy indoctrination, and manipulation. This research investigates the presence of potential data voids surrounding United States, political topics within the Latinx-oriented Facebook communities (pew research).

    Data voids that have been explicitly researched are short tail, meaning that a lack of information is already acknowledged and more information will be produced to clear up any confusion surrounding that topic. Alternatively, this research investigates long tail data voids, where a lack of information persists and is unaddressed excaberating particular ideologies.

    Data incorporated for this study comes from Crowdtangle, a public insights tool owned and operated by Facebook.
